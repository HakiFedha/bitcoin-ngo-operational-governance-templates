# Template: Bitcoin Communication Kit for Donors and Boards

## How to use this template
This kit contains ready-to-adapt language for three common situations: introducing Bitcoin to your board, updating donors on your Bitcoin policy, and responding to questions or concerns. Adjust the bracketed sections for your organisation. Remove sections that do not apply.

> 💡 **Before you begin:** If you need strategic advice on how to navigate these conversations, handle skepticism, or frame the civil society and human rights case for Bitcoin, read our companion guide [**How to Communicate Bitcoin to Donors and Boards**](https://hakifedha.org/docs/guides/bitcoin-guide/communication-guide).

## Document 1 — Board Memo: Proposal to Accept Bitcoin

**To:** Board of Directors  
**From:** `[Name]`, `[Title]`  
**Date:** `[Date]`  
**Subject:** Proposal to accept Bitcoin as an additional funding channel  

### Summary
This memo proposes that `[Organisation Name]` begin accepting Bitcoin donations alongside existing funding channels. This is not a proposal to hold Bitcoin as an investment or to replace our existing financial infrastructure. It is a proposal to add a resilient, censorship-resistant channel for receiving funds that operates independently of the banking system.

### Why we are considering this
`[Organisation Name]` operates in an environment where financial access cannot be taken for granted. Across Africa, civil society organisations have experienced bank account freezes, blocked cross-border transfers, and donor fund restrictions that have paralysed operations without any legal process. Bitcoin provides a funding channel that no bank, payment processor, or government institution can block.

Beyond resilience, Bitcoin also enables us to receive funds from international supporters who face their own barriers to conventional cross-border transfers, and from donors who wish to give anonymously.

### What we are proposing
We propose to accept Bitcoin donations through `[BTCPay Server / a custodial donation platform / our self-custody wallet — select as applicable]`. `[%]` of incoming Bitcoin will be converted to local currency within `[number]` days of receipt unless the board resolves otherwise and `[%]` may be held in Bitcoin. Bitcoin will not be held as a speculative investment.

Governance controls will follow our Bitcoin Governance Framework, requiring `[number]` authorised signatories to approve any Bitcoin transaction above `[threshold amount in local currency]`.

### What this requires from the board
*   Board approval of this proposal.
*   Designation of `[number]` key holders from senior staff or board members.
*   Review and approval of the Bitcoin Governance Framework and custody policy.

### Risks and how we will manage them
*   **Price volatility:** Managed by converting to local currency or stablecoin promptly after receipt.
*   **Operational error:** Managed by multisig custody requiring multiple approvals and regular wallet audits.
*   **Regulatory uncertainty:** We have reviewed current guidance in `[country]`. See the attached summary. We will monitor regulatory developments and report to the board quarterly.
*   **Reputational concern:** Bitcoin is increasingly used by established international NGOs, foundations, and human rights organisations. We are prepared to explain our policy clearly to any donor or partner who raises concerns.

### Recommendation
We recommend the board approve this proposal and authorise the Executive Director to implement the Bitcoin Governance Framework as presented.

## Document 2 — Donor Update: Our Bitcoin Policy

**Subject:** How `[Organisation Name]` handles Bitcoin donations  

Dear `[Donor name / Friends of Organisation Name]`,

We want to be transparent about how we manage Bitcoin donations and why we have chosen to accept them.

### Why we accept Bitcoin
Financial access for civil society in Africa is not guaranteed. We have seen peer organisations lose access to their funds through bank account freezes, blocked transfers, and donor restrictions, often without warning and without any legal basis. Bitcoin gives us a funding channel that operates outside the banking system and cannot be blocked by any institution.

We also recognise that some of our supporters face their own barriers to conventional international transfers, or prefer to give without creating a financial record. Bitcoin accommodates both.

### How we handle Bitcoin we receive
All Bitcoin received is recorded immediately with the local currency equivalent at the time of receipt. We hold Bitcoin for a maximum of `[number]` days before converting to local currency for operational use, unless market conditions make immediate conversion significantly disadvantageous to the organisation. We do not hold Bitcoin as a speculative investment.

All Bitcoin transactions require approval from `[number]` authorised signatories under our governance framework. We maintain a full accounting record of all Bitcoin received, converted, and spent, which is available to donors on request and included in our annual financial statements.

### Tax and compliance
We have taken legal advice on our obligations in `[country]` and are satisfied that our Bitcoin accounting practices meet applicable reporting requirements. A summary of our compliance approach is available on request.

### Questions
If you have any questions about our Bitcoin policy, please contact `[name]` at `[email]`. We are also happy to arrange a call if you would like to discuss this in more detail.

## Document 3 — FAQ: Bitcoin Questions from Donors and Partners

#### Q: Is Bitcoin legal in `[country]`?
**A:** Yes. Bitcoin is legal to hold and use in `[country]`. `[Add brief country-specific note here.]` We have taken legal advice on our obligations and are satisfied that our use of Bitcoin complies with applicable law.

#### Q: Is Bitcoin not used for money laundering?
**A:** This is a common misconception. Bitcoin transactions are recorded permanently on a public ledger and are more traceable than cash. Major international organisations, foundations, and governments hold and use Bitcoin. Our own Bitcoin activity is fully recorded and auditable.

#### Q: How do you protect against Bitcoin's price volatility?
**A:** We convert Bitcoin to local currency within `[number]` days of receipt or stablecoin. We do not hold Bitcoin as an investment. The value we record for accounting purposes is the local currency equivalent at the time of receipt.

#### Q: Can I give Bitcoin anonymously?
**A:** Yes. Bitcoin donations do not require you to provide personal information to us. If you wish to give anonymously, simply send Bitcoin to our donation address. If you require a donation receipt for your own records, please contact us, and we will discuss how to accommodate that while respecting your privacy preferences.

#### Q: How do I know my Bitcoin donation reached you?
**A:** Every Bitcoin transaction is recorded permanently on the public blockchain. When you send a donation, you receive a transaction ID that you can verify independently at any Bitcoin block explorer. We will also acknowledge receipt by email if you provide a contact address.

#### Q: What happens if Bitcoin becomes illegal or heavily regulated in your country?
**A:** We monitor the regulatory environment continuously and take legal advice on significant developments. If the regulatory situation changes materially, we will update our Bitcoin policy and communicate that to donors promptly.
